Types of Scholarships

Merit Scholarships

Merit scholarships are not only for academic excellence. They recognize people of different ethic backgrounds with a broad range of talents. Merit scholarships are considered very prestigious as they are often national awards.

Need-Based Scholarship

A need-based scholarship is awarded based on you or your parents’ financial background. The factors considered when assessing your financial status are income, cost of living, and the number of children enrolled in school in your household.

Minority Scholarships

Some of these are for all minorities as a collective whole, while others are catered to individual ethnic groups.

Athletic Scholarships

Athletic scholarships are usually full-ride scholarships for top-notch athletes to be recruited to universities’ athletic teams. Athletes are required to balance their studies with rigorous athletic training. Athletes are usually selected by scouts while in high-school.

School-Based Individual Scholarships

Educational institutions usually offer scholarships for academic merit, financial need, or certain athletic teams. A school may also provide financial aid to a specific program, such as engineering scholarships or nursing scholarships. You may be required to apply for an individual scholarship or you may be awarded upon acceptance to the school.

Military Scholarships

There are college scholarships available for families with service members or veterans of the Air Force, Army, Coast Guard, Marine Corps, or Navy.

Miscsellaneous Scholarships

These scholarships are for students with unusual talents and interests. They include art scholarships, writing scholarships, and other random scholarship opportunities. They are typically sponsored by private companies and are meant to be fun.
